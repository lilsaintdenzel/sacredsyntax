Gethsemane means "oil press."

A garden on the Mount of Olives where olives were crushed to extract oil. Where pressure revealed what was inside. Where Jesus went to pray the night before his crucifixion, knowing what was coming.

This is where the weight became real.

But he stayed. And he prayed. "Not my will, but yours be done."

This is the moment that changes everything.

Because Gethsemane is where obedience was chosen in the face of unbearable cost. Where love proved stronger than fear. Where the Son of God wrestled with what it meant to be fully human and fully divine.

We often skip this part. We go straight from Palm Sunday to Good Friday, from triumph to execution. But Gethsemane is where the real battle was fought. In prayer. In submission. In the crushing weight of what was to come. The King who would wear a crown of thorns instead of a royal diadem.

The oil press. The place where everything inside is brought to the surface. Where there's nowhere to hide. Where the truth of what you're made of is revealed under pressure.

THE GARDEN WHERE
SURRENDER BECAME VICTORY

Gethsemane reminds us that obedience is costly. That following Jesus means going to your own garden, facing your own crushing weight, and choosing "not my will, but yours." It's where we learn that the Christian life isn't about avoiding suffering but about meeting it with faith. About staying when everything in us wants to run.
