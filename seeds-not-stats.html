seeds, not stats:
the kingdom metric.
Faithfulness over forerunners.

In a world where the forerunner seems to be statistics as Kingdom citizens we should be more mindful of the seeds we sow and not the stats we project.

Likes. Views. Followers. Engagement rates. The algorithms reward what's trending, what's viral, what's measurable. But the Kingdom of God operates on a completely different economy.

Even the issue of statistics is nothing new. David had his fair share of being mindful of numbers, and we see in Scripture how it affected the course of events.

But God's economy doesn't run on metrics. It runs on faithfulness. On obedience. On small, hidden acts of planting that no dashboard will ever track.

"Unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit." — John 12:24

Seeds, not stats.

God's eventuality always turns seeds into fruits, which in turn birth more seeds—and the resulting harvest is beyond what we can imagine.

"The kingdom of heaven is like a grain of mustard seed that a man took and sowed in his field. It is the smallest of all seeds, but when it has grown it is larger than all the garden plants." — Matthew 13:31-32

The question isn't how many people saw it. The question is: did you plant it in faith? Did you water it with prayer? Did you trust God with the growth?

"I planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the growth." — 1 Corinthians 3:6
